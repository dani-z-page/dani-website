Who I work with

Leadership teams with plenty of data—and no shared answer.

I work with founder-led, investor-backed, and PE-backed B2B SaaS companies where growth has become harder to explain.

The company may have product analytics, customer research, pipeline reviews, support themes, churn analysis, and experienced executives. But Product, Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success are still operating from different versions of the customer.

—The company is post-product-market-fit, but growth has stalled or become volatile.
—Trial, conversion, retention, NRR, or expansion is underperforming.
—Product and GTM disagree about the ICP or the customer's most important jobs.
—Self-serve and sales-assisted growth do not connect cleanly.
—The company has meaningful customer data but no coherent growth diagnosis.
—Leadership has an AI mandate but no grounded view of where AI will create measurable value.
—The problem is too cross-functional for one department to own alone.

The pattern

Everyone has a theory. Each theory lives in someone else's department.

01Marketing is generating more sign-ups, but too few become successful customers.
02Product is responding to the largest and loudest accounts while broader market needs go unmet.
03Sales, Product, and Customer Success are working from different definitions of the ideal customer.
04Customer Success is absorbing friction that should have been solved in acquisition, onboarding, or the product.
05NRR is under pressure, but leadership cannot agree whether the cause is fit, value, service, pricing, or execution.
06AI initiatives are multiplying without a clear connection to customer or business outcomes.

These are not six separate problems. They are signs that the company is not operating around one coherent understanding of the customer journey.

The point of view

Who is buying fills the funnel. Who is succeeding determines whether growth holds.

Marketing sees who responds. Sales sees who will buy. Product sees what active users do. Customer Success hears from established accounts. Support sees customers at the moment of friction.

Each view is useful. None is the whole customer. I connect those signals to identify which customers succeed, why others fail to realize value, and what the company should target, build, change, automate, or stop.

Otherwise, new logos simply backfill churn—and the topline calls it growth.

The method · The Growth Operating System

A practical operating model for finding—and fixing—the constraints behind growth.

It is not a departmental audit or a generic playbook. It is a way to identify the small number of constraints limiting activation, conversion, retention, and expansion—and organize the company around fixing them.

01 · EXPERIENCE THE JOURNEY

Leadership experiences the journey directly: how customers discover the company, interpret the promise, evaluate the product, reach value, seek help, expand, and decide whether to stay.

02 · CONNECT THE SIGNALS

Customer interviews, product behavior, acquisition data, sales conversations, support patterns, retention, expansion, and customer economics become one decision model.

03 · FIND THE CONSTRAINT

The team identifies the few cross-functional constraints that explain the growth pattern and separates root causes from symptoms.

04 · BUILD AND TRANSFER

The diagnosis becomes a sequenced operating plan with clear ownership, measures, decision points, and execution support.
